Sinhagad or Kondana is a pride of the southwestern region of Pune. This fort was the one to support Maharashtra during the ancient battles. Bhuleshwar range of the great Sahyadris is the home to this historical monument. It is located at an elevation of 1,312 m above sea level. Sinhagad has got its own breakneck slopes to protect it from all sides.

There are two entrances to this fort – one in the north-eastern region (Pune Door) and another one towards the southeastern part (Kalyan Door). Some of the sources say that the fort could have been built over 2000 years ago. The fort was formerly known as Kondhana, which has witnessed many battles through the centuries, and the most important one was the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670.

This is evident from the carvings present on the walls of Kaundinyeshwar temple there. Sinhagad is valued as a true sightseeing heritage. The fortress displays some of the adorable monoliths of Subhedar Tanaji Malusare – one of the efficient safe-keepers of the fort from Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj’s era. Pune Darwaza, Kalyan Darwaza, Tilak Bungalow, Hawa Point, Kade Lot, Amruteshwar Temple, Subhedar Tanaji Samadhi and Memorial raises the elegance of this fortress even today.
