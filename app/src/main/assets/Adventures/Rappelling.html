Maharashtra, one of the most sought after travel destinations, is ideal for experiencing thrills of some of the most wild and whacky adventure sports.Rappelling is one such adventure sport that tops the things to do list of countless adventure freaks.

In Maharashtra the Konkan Kada cliff at Harishchandragad, Dukes Nose Lonavala, Sandhan Valley near Ratangad and Karjat near Mumbai are best destinations to indulge in this madcap adventure sport. To enter the next level of excitement, one can also try their hands on waterfall rappelling. Characterizing extreme challenges, one can indulge in waterfall rappelling at Kundalika River, Bhivpuri, Kondane Caves, Panshet and Diksal.

There are numerous options that available in the state, to tone down the craze of adventure enthusiasts. Rappelling, all it requires is fine balance and a strong foothold. One has to be ready to face the dangerous descends, and focus is the key, when the steps become wobbly. As the state is blessed with numerous waterfalls, this adventure sport can be best enjoyed in the monsoons.

Sandhan Valley :

It is one of the most difficult rappelling point in Maharashtra. Enjoy the breath-taking view of mighty ranges like Alang, Madan, Kulang, Ratangad, Ajoba and Kalsubai peak from the summit. This 200 feet deep and about 1.5 km long water-carved valleys offer 45 feet rappelling at multiple points.

Kundalika River Rappelling :

Embark on an unforgettable adventure by going waterfall rappelling near Kundalika River! Rappel down the craggy outcrops of the Sahyadri Mountains attached to a harness and make your way through a waterfall. Let the rushing waterfall provide you with a rare and memorable experience as you engage in the offbeat sport of waterfall rappelling.
