The Bhaja Caves are a medley of rock-cut 22 caves located near Lonavala in Pune, Maharashtra. These caves are located about 400 ft. above the Bhaja Village. It is located on the vital trading route from Arabian Sea to Deccan. These caves have stupas, which is one of their vital features. The caves comprise of the chaityagrihas and the viharas.

The chaityagriha boasts of amazing horseshoe ceiling and splendid wooden architecture. It even has some commendable images of Buddha. The viharas of these caves have pillared verandas, which are decorated with exclusive relief.

These caves are popular for their awe-inspiring wooden architecture. There are fascinating carvings on these caves. The architectural design of these caves is similar to that of the Karla Caves. The sculptures of these caves exhibit elaborate garlands, headdress and jewellery.
