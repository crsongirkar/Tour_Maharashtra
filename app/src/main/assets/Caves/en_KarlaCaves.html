These Karla Caves, popularly known as Karla Cells are a conglomeration of enticing rock-cut caves of Buddhist origin situated at Karli close to Lonavala in the state of Maharashtra. These caves are some of the largest rock-cut Buddhist shrines in India. These shrines were constructed in the period between 2nd-5th centuries. One of the oldest cave shrines belongs to the 160BC period.

Karla Caves are very famous tourist destination because of their amazing architecture. Inscriptions and Stupas in the caves depict the ancient Buddhist architecture and culture. The main cave consists of a chaitya hall that has 37 octagonal pillars and a base of water jar. The hall has many beautifully decorated sculptures of men, women and animals.

All around the cave complex, there are many chaityas and viharas. A Vihara is a place where the monks used to stay and meditate in the caves for a long period of time. The early Buddhist school, Mahasamghika, is associated with these caves. Their popularity in this part of the region was wide-spread.
