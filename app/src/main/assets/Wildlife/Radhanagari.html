In the shadow of the western ghat lies the Radhanagari Wildlife Sanctuary, which is home to more than 600 Bisons and various types of rich flora and fauna. Also known as the Dajipur Sanctuary, the park was formed in the year 1985 and immediately rose to fame due to its location. Accredited as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. The Sanctuary which is spread over 351 sq. km is home to a wide range of flora and fauna. Here we can see bison, tiger, leopard, sloth bear, Giant Squirrel, Mouse Deer, Barking Deer, and so on.

Additionally, this is located in the Sahyadri Range and is the abode of many reptiles, birds, and animals. Tributaries of river Krishna, Bhogavati, Tulshi, Dirba Pass through the Sanctuary which provides water to the animals and birds.

In the park, there are two dams; one constructed on Bhogavati river (Radhanagari Dam) and other on river Dudhganga (Kallamwadi Dam). These two dams attract numberless bird species like vultures, eagles, jungle fowl, quails, plovers, sandpipers, owlets, doves, owls, nightjars, to name a few. To witness the beauty of the national park, one should visit in the morning and evening.

Location: Located at Kolhapur district of Maharashtra.

Best Time to visit: The sanctuary remains closed between June and October. Best time to visit the sanctuary is during winters (November to March).
