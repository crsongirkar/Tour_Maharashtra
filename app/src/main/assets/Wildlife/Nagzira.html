Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary is another important Maharashtra wildlife sanctuaries. Nagzira Sanctuary which is also known as the open outdoor museum is home to many flora and fauna. The name Nagzira came from ‘Nag’(Snake) temple of Mahadeva which is in the middle of the sanctuary, and 'zira' (zara) in Marathi means a perennial source of water. Nagzira wildlife sanctuary is located between Bhandara-Gondia district of Maharashtra.

Nawegaon-Nagzira Tiger Reserve (NNTR) was notified as 46 tiger reserve of India in December, 2013 and It's 5 Tiger reserve of Maharashtra. One can find mammals, birds and different other invertebrates in the sanctuary. The Nagzira Wildlife sanctuary is famous for tiger, leopard, sambar, wild dog, cheetah, mouse deer and many other forms of wildlife and it is one of the best wildlife sanctuaries in Maharashtra.

Best time to visit: The best time to visit the sanctuary is between October and June. The sanctuary remains closed during the monsoons.
